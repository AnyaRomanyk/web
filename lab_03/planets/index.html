10 Interesting Facts About the Moon

The dark side of the moon is a myth.

The Moon is tidally locked to the Earth, which means we always see the same side of the Moon, but it still has a day and night cycle.

The rise and fall of tides on Earth is caused by the Moon.

The Moon's gravitational pull affects Earth's oceans, causing high and low tides.

The Moon is drifting away from the Earth.

The Moon moves away from Earth by about 3.8 centimeters per year due to tidal interactions.

A person would weigh much less on the Moon.

Due to the Moon's lower gravity (about 1/6th of Earth's), a person would weigh much less on the Moon.

The Moon has only been walked on by 12 people, all American males.

Between 1969 and 1972, 12 astronauts, all from the USA, walked on the Moon during the Apollo missions.

The Moon has no atmosphere.

Without an atmosphere, there is no weather on the Moon, and the sky always appears black, even during the day.

The Moon has quakes.

Known as "moonquakes," these tremors are caused by gravitational interactions with Earth.

The first spacecraft to reach the Moon was Luna 1 in 1959.

Luna 1, a Soviet spacecraft, was the first to reach the Moon in 1959, although it did not land.

The Moon will be visited by man in the near future.

NASA's Artemis program plans to return astronauts to the Moon by the mid-2020s, including the first woman.

During the 1950s, the USA considered detonating a nuclear bomb on the Moon.

In the 1950s, the U.S. military considered a project to detonate a nuclear bomb on the Moon to demonstrate power during the Cold War.
